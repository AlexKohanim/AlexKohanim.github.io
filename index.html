About

I am a Software Developer with a A.S. in Computer Science from Santa Barbara City College and a B.S. in Computer Science from San Francisco State University. You can find my work history in the resume on the home page. I started my developemt career in High School when I joined a newly added Computer Science class offered by the school, at the same time I was enrolled in the robotics class. In both of these classes I learned a good amount of basic Java. Shortly after joining the robotics class, I was able to lead the electrical team. Here I lead a team of 12 students to discuss and plan for the building of the electrical components of our robot. I was fortunate enough to have a strong team making my day to day easier. The class also had a program for helping local Boys&Girls clubs (Grades 1-5) by teaching them some basics of programming and building ethernet cables. Additionally, I lead sessions for my underclassmen which included the basics of soldering.

After High School, I transfered to Santa Barbara City College. This is where my programming journey really picked up. Here I learned a good amount more of Java, C/C++, HTML/CSS/JS, Arm Assembly, Digital Logic Design, Discrete Math, Android, Python and how to work as a team. While I was at SBCC, I joined and ran the Computer Science Club which has been around since the 80s. It was a lot of pressure stepping in after the previous president as they set the expectaions high. Under my term, the club had a good amount of social, networking and workshop events. One of my favioites that we did 2 semesters in a row was touring a local company, this allowed myself and the student see what it might look like actually working after college. I fell in love with Computer Science while at SBCC, I really struggled to work on my other classes since I was having so much fun working on and expanding my projects. I became a Lab TA as well, which ment I became a tutor for all the classes offered by all the professors, this gave me a whole new insight into the basics that I could never of asked for. I was also responsible for any technical issues involved in the lab, when tended to be minimal. I became a grader for a few of the professors too during my time there, this gave me a great insight into different students difficulties on many topics, and different approches to the same problems I had never thought of. It also opened my mind to the idea of automation since many classes used a graded unit test system. while grading, I would collobrate with the professors to modify the assignments and add new ones as we got creative.
